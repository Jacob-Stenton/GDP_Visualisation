What

This is a visualisation project focusing on annual gross domestic product (GDP), total energy consumption and the average household price of energy in the United Kingdom. The bulk of this project was completed using d3.js, which is a library used for creating data visualisations. Clicking on the link above will take you to a few graphs representing data linked to the previously mentioned topics. They offer a small, but important, analytical insight into the economic state of the UK.

Why

It's important to examine these subjects closely as they help us understand our country's economic health. GDP reflects our overall economic activity, which then informs important policy descisions made by our government. Energy usage helps indicate industrial developement, as well as our sustainability. Energy prices impact inflation, cost of living, and industrial competitiveness, making it an important factor to monitor as it helps to ensure economic stability and social equity. These indicators collectively provide insights into the economy's performance, helping to build new ideas for efficient, fair and sustainable growth.

Data

Three different data sets are used in this project. Each data set has a different range of dates, hinderng the range of data that can be used on some of the graphs.

Data:

Gross domestic product of the United Kingdom from 1948 to 2023

Energy use: total

Average annual domestic electricity bills by home and non-home supplier (QEP 2.2.1)
